First Step Towards Quantum Physics

A journey from everyday wonder to the strange, fundamental laws that govern our universe.

The Wonder of "Why?"

We see the world; we see raindrops falling down, plants growing upward, bolts of lightning illuminating the sky, the apparent motion of the Sun around the Earth, the phases of the Moon, the reactions of different chemicals, the evaporation of water, the working of computers, satellites launching into space, clouds moving across the sky, air conditioners expelling cool air, telescopes zooming into the craters of the Moon, microscopes examining the dust of the Moon, rainbows forming in the sky, tornadoes swirling over the oceans, and our fingertips wrinkling after being placed in water for a long time.

Then we wonder "why" - why raindrops fall, why some things react while others do not, why rainbows form, why the universe even exists, and why life itself exists. This sense of wonder and curiosity drives us to seek the answers to our "whys" and "hows."

Diving Deeper: The Scientific Approach

But how can we find these answers? The most intuitive and reasonable way is to dive deeper. If you see a very complex machine working, you would first remove its outer cover, examine the function of each component, and then ponder the interactions among them. This would be the most logical way to understand the mechanism and purpose of that machine.

Similarly, this approach has guided scientists from the ancient to the modern era. It was not without reason that Democritus conceived of atoms, Dalton experimented to prove them, Thomson sought to understand them, Planck developed the concept of quanta, Einstein explained the photoelectric effect using particles of light called photons, Schr√∂dinger described the behavior of quantum particles, and Bernoulli developed the kinetic theory of gases to explain the behavior of molecules.

This process continues today in the discoveries of the Higgs boson and neutrinos. All of this was done to understand how everything works by understanding the behavior of the fundamental building blocks that make up larger systems.

A New, "Naughty" Reality

Surprisingly, this exploration transformed our entire viewpoint of reality. At that microscopic level, matter behaves in ways that defy ordinary comprehension.

The very essence of this "naughty" micro-world is that it does not behave like the familiar macroscopic world.

It exhibits properties many interpret as incomprehensible, such as spin, uncertainty in position and momentum, and wave-particle duality. This study, later named "Quantum Mechanics," dared to explore the realm of the extremely strange and unified many theories under its umbrella.

The Challenge of Interpretation

Along with these theories came multiple interpretations of the same physical phenomena, which continue to challenge physicists due to the lack of decisive evidence and the remarkable consistency of each interpretation with experimental results up to this day.

The Potent Medicine for Curiosity

In conclusion, quantum mechanics was formulated to understand the behavior and interactions of the smallest constituents of matter, thereby uncovering the "whys" and "hows" underlying the laws of physics and the behavior of macroscopic objects.

Ultimately, physics exists to cure our eternal disease of curiosity, and quantum mechanics remains the most potent medicine for this ailment of wonder.

Final Thought: Yet, as with every effective medicine, it is often bitter in taste.

  • Physics, and especially quantum mechanics, is the "potent medicine" for our curiosity, even if its concepts are challenging to grasp.
